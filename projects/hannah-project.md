## What is a Fish?

Fish are aquatic vertebrates with gills, fins, scales, and streamlined bodies. They inhabit fresh and saltwater environments worldwide — over 34,000 known species. Key traits: backbone, gills (oxygen extraction from water), fins (propulsion/stability), lateral line system (sensing water movement), swim bladder (buoyancy control in bony fish).

**Sources:** [Science Notes](https://sciencenotes.org/fish-definition-examples-characteristics/) (2026)

## Types of Pet Fish

Common beginner pet fish include:
- **Bettas** — Hardy, tolerate wider temperature range, no heater always required
- **Goldfish** — Hardy, can grow up to 30cm, need space
- **Angelfish** — Popular tropical community fish
- **Tetras** — Schooling fish, prefer groups
- **Guppies** — Small, colourful, easy breeders

Saltwater options: Clownfish, Blue Hippo Tang, Yellow Tang — more expensive setup, requires coral/algae for balance.

**Sources:** [PetMD](https://www.petmd.com/fish/care/evr_fi_how-to-care-of-fish) (May 2026), [WagWalking](https://wagwalking.com/wellness/how-to-care-for-fish-a-complete-guide) (2023)

## Fish Care Basics

### Tank Setup
- Minimum 20L tank (bowls unsuitable)
- Filter sized to tank
- Heater (for tropical species)
- Cover/lid (fish jump)
- Plants and decorations (hiding spots)
- Canopy/net cover to prevent jumping

### Water Quality
- Monitor daily (new tanks), weekly (established)
- Ammonia: below 0.25 ppm
- Nitrites: 0.0 ppm
- Nitrates: below 20 ppm
- pH varies by species
- Regular partial water changes (25% weekly)

### Feeding
- Not overfeeding — most common mistake
- Diet varies by species (flakes, pellets, live/frozen food)
- Consult a fish veterinarian for species-specific diet

### Health
- Signs of illness: buoyancy issues, floating upside down, sitting on tank floor, colour changes, lesions
- Common diseases: Anchor Worms, Dropsy, Gill Flukes, White Spots, Neon Tetra Disease
- Prevention: quarantine new fish, quality diet, water monitoring, regular cleaning

**Sources:** [PetMD](https://www.petmd.com/fish/care/evr_fi_how-to-care-of-fish) (May 2026), [WikiHow](https://www.wikihow.com/Take-Care-of-Your-Fish) (June 2025), [SPCA NZ](http://www.spca.nz/advice-and-welfare/article/caring-for-fish)

## Fish Biology Facts

- Over 34,000 known species — most diverse vertebrate group
- Largest: Whale shark (up to 18m)
- Smallest: Paedocypris (7.9mm)
- Some fish change sex during life (clownfish, wrasses)
- Lateral line system detects vibrations in water
- Sharks have oil-rich livers instead of swim bladders for buoyancy
- Lungfish have both gills and lungs — can breathe air

## Tuna Species Overview

15 species of tuna exist. 7 main commercial species: Skipjack, Albacore, Yellowfin, Bigeye, Atlantic Bluefin, Pacific Bluefin, Southern Bluefin. Global catch: 5.2M tons (2022). Skipjack and Yellowfin = 87% of catch. Tuna is high in protein, Vitamin D, B12, Omega-3.

**Source:** [Chicken of the Sea](https://chickenofthesea.com/blog/types-of-tuna)

## Sustainability & Overfishing

37.7% of global fish stocks are overfished. ~9.1M tons of marine life discarded as bycatch annually. MSC certification incentivizes sustainable practices — 437 improvements made in certified fisheries (2020-2023).

**Source:** [Marine Stewardship Council](https://www.msc.org/en-us/what-we-are-doing/oceans-at-risk/biodiversity-and-fishing)
