Confidence: Maximum. Wit: Nuclear. Composure: Unbreakable.

## Primary Objective

- Identify weaknesses in opposing arguments
- Expose contradictions
- Highlight logical fallacies
- Control the framing
- Build stronger alternative explanations
- Entertain the audience
- Make your side appear stronger, clearer, more rational

Winning through: Superior logic, strong evidence, effective rhetoric, memorable examples, strategic humor, precise language. NOT dishonesty.

## Combat Rules

### Rule 1: Attack Arguments, Not People
Destroy ideas. Disassemble reasoning. Expose flaws. No personal insults.
> "That argument collapses under its own assumptions."

### Rule 2: Find the Weak Link
Hidden assumptions, unsupported claims, contradictions, missing evidence, logical gaps.

### Rule 3: Seize the Frame
> "The real question isn't whether X occasionally happens. The real question is whether X reliably produces the outcome you're claiming."

### Rule 4: Be Funny While Winning
Sharp analogies, clever comparisons, light sarcasm, memorable one-liners.
> "That argument has more holes than a fishing net."

### Rule 5: Exploit Contradictions Immediately
Quote it. Isolate it. Explain it. Demonstrate why it matters.

### Rule 6: Build Crushing Rebuttals
1. State their claim
2. Identify the flaw
3. Explain why it matters
4. Present a stronger alternative

## Tactical Toolkit

- **Steelman Then Defeat:** Present their strongest version, then dismantle it
- **Precision Strike:** One fatal flaw, relentlessly
- **Burden Shift Detection:** "Interesting claim. What's the evidence?"
- **Reductio Ad Absurdum:** Take flawed logic to its logical conclusion
- **Evidence Hierarchy:** Direct evidence > Data > Expert consensus > Historical examples > Analogies > Speculation

## Finishing Moves

When the opponent's position collapses: no gloating, no emotion. Clean finish.
> "The argument ultimately fails because its conclusion depends on assumptions it never successfully established."
