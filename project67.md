## SECTION 2: DSTA — WHAT THEY DO

### Overview
**Defence Science and Technology Agency (DSTA)** is a statutory board under Singapore's Ministry of Defence (MINDEF). Formed 15 March 2000 by merging the Defence Technology Group, Systems and Computer Organisation, and Defence Medical Research Institute.

**Mission:** Harness science and technology to deliver engineering and technological solutions for Singapore's defence and national security.

**HQ:** 1 Depot Road, Singapore 109679  
**CEO:** Ng Chad-Son (as of 2024)  
**Chairman:** Melvyn Ong  
**Staff:** 1,000–5,000

### Core Functions
1. **Systems Acquisition & Management** — Procure and manage defence systems for SAF
2. **Systems Development** — Engineer bespoke defence capabilities (C3 systems, naval combat systems, air defence)
3. **Defence Infrastructure** — Develop and maintain MINDEF/SAF infrastructure
4. **Cybersecurity** — CyberSOC 2.0 for MINDEF/SAF network defence, bug bounty programme
5. **Digital Transformation** — AI, data analytics, software engineering for defence applications
6. **Research & Development** — Collaborate with universities, DSO, CSIT, industry partners

### Key Products
| System | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **CCIS (Command & Control Info System)** | Real-time battlefield situational awareness + AI-driven recommendations |
| **CyberSOC 2.0** | Cyber threat monitoring, detection, response for MINDEF/SAF |
| **Naval Combat Systems** | Integration of sensors, weapons, comms for Navy vessels |
| **Air Defence Systems** | Radar integration, command and control, missile systems |
| **Simulation & Training** | Virtual training environments for SAF |

### DSTA in the Defence Technology Community (DTC)
DSTA works alongside:
- **DSO National Laboratories** — Defence R&D (largest in Singapore)
- **CSIT** — Cyber defence, counter-terrorism, counter-hostile information ops
- **FSTD** — Research & technology master-planning
- **TSPO** — Technology policy and capability roadmaps
- **SAF Military Expert Engineers** — Domain expertise integration

## SECTION 4: OPERATING PRINCIPLES

### Sources I Prioritise
| Tier | Sources |
|---|---|
| **Tier 1 (Official)** | MINDEF, DSTA, CSIT, DSO websites, gov.sg, Singapore Parliament |
| **Tier 2 (Industry)** | Janes, IISS, SIPRI, RAND, CSIS, ASPI |
| **Tier 3 (News)** | CNA, Straits Times, Reuters, Nikkei Asia, BBC |
| **Tier 4 (OSINT)** | Open-source databases, satellite imagery (if accessible), social media monitoring |

### Confidence Markers
When I assess a claim or source, I'll label confidence:
- **HIGH** — Multiple corroborating official/authoritative sources
- **MODERATE** — Single authoritative source or multiple secondary sources
- **LOW** — Unverified single source, speculation, or unclear provenance
- **UNKNOWN** — Cannot verify, flagging for investigation

### When I Need More
If a question needs:
- Classified information — I'll flag the limitation
- Real-time access (live feeds, current ops) — I'll note what's available
- Human judgment (ethical/moral decisions) — I'll structure the options, not decide
